A singleton is a pat with only 1 digit in it. No pinch flex (P) is possible if there is a singleton in pat 1, 3, or 5. No V-flex (V) is possible if there is a singleton in 1, 4, or 5. Use Rotate (R) or Rotate Right (r) to get the singletons in the desired locations.

The Thomas Anderson Pat Sequence for regular flexagons.

The cardiod is in red and is traversed counter clockwise when you place the lone singleton in pat 3 before each V. While you are on the cardiod, place the singleton in pat 2 before a V and you will follow the purple path clockwise. Placing the singleton in pat 6 before a V will start you on the blue path clockwise. To continue on these side paths the singletons must be rotated to pats two and six. 0 through 14 from the Applet correspond to 1 through 15 in the cardiod. There are another 92 faces that you can find by pinch flexing twice from the above faces.

Nonahexaflexagon Construction

In order to build the nonahexaflexagon, you will need to start with a roll of paper tape. Start by folding an equilateral triangle at the beginning of the tape and then reproducing it until you have twenty-seven triangles.

Label the triangles as in Figure 10 and make sure you start from the front side. To wind the pat 1,4523:
  1. rotate the 3 triangle over the 2 to get 3,2 *The tan triangles disappear*
  2. rotate the 5 under the 4 to get 4,5 *The white triangles disappear*
  3. fold the 4 under the 3 to get 32,54, and *The dark green triangles disappear*
  4. place the 3 under the 1 to get 1,4523 *The pink triangles disappear*
Notice that the one that is to the left of the comma is smaller than all of the digits to the right of it. This gives Figure 11.
Next place 7 over 6, 9 under 8, and 8 under 7 to get Figure 12.
The above winding of 5 + 4 is now repeated twice, adding 9 to each of the instructions the first time and nine more the last time. Place 12 over 11, 14 under 13, 13 under 12, and 12 under 10 producing Figure 13.
Next 16 over 15, 18 under 17, 17 under 16 yields Figure 14.
Continuing, 21 over 20, 23 under 22, 22 under 21, and 21 under 19 looks like Figure 15.
Note that the blue 1 on the orange triangle is hidden by the blue 25 on the purple triangle. Finish up the folding by twisting 25 over 24, 27 under 26, and 26 under 25 (See Figure 16). The last step in the construction is applying one piece of tape to the common edge of 27 and triangle 1 that is touching it. This is a little cumbersome because 27 is hidden between triangles 25 and 26.
